STYLE BIBLE

Core Principle

Courtside Chaos should feel like a forgotten sports-comedy classic discovered on VHS.

Not modern.

Not corporate.

Not data-driven.

Not sleek.

The player should feel like they’re running a basketball team from a cluttered office above a leaking arena.

⸻

Visual Inspirations

Sports Illustrated (1980s)

Large photography.

Human stories.

Local heroes.

Small-town legends.

⸻

Atari & Early EA Box Art

Dramatic painted scenes.

Characters larger than life.

Action frozen in time.

Bold typography.

⸻

Local Newspapers

Headline-driven storytelling.

Black-and-white photos.

Community focus.

Minor scandals treated as major news.

⸻

Sierra Adventure Games

Character portraits.

Warm humour.

Memorable personalities.

Readable interfaces.

⸻

Cinemaware

Presentation over simulation.

Big moments.

Big characters.

Big stories.

⸻

Things We Avoid

No Modern Sports UI

Avoid:

* giant stat dashboards
* radar charts
* analytics screens
* advanced metrics
* salary-cap spreadsheets

If it looks like a modern sports app, remove it.

⸻

No Pixel-Art Dependency

Pixel art may appear later.

It is not the identity.

The identity is:

Painted Americana.

1980s sports illustration.

Local newspaper photography.

⸻

No Absurd Fantasy

Not:

* aliens
* supernatural events
* magic
* zombies
* talking animals

Everything must be believable.

Funny because it could happen.

⸻

User Interface Philosophy

Sandra First

Sandra is the interface.

The player should hear from Sandra more often than they see menus.

Bad:

Financial Report

Good:

Boss, we’re down to our last operational coffee machine.

⸻

Story Before Numbers

Whenever possible:

Story first.

Numbers second.

Example:

Bad:

Attendance: 2,163

Good:

Attendance was surprisingly strong despite the roof leak near Section C.

⸻

Newspapers Matter

Newspapers are a core reward.

Every week should generate headlines.

Examples:

Flamingos Survive Beer Night Disaster

Mascot Questionable For Friday

Malone Misses Bus Again

Tiny McGee Wins Local Election

⸻

Writing Style

Sandra

Dry.

Smart.

Understated.

Never mean.

Examples:

Boss, the mascot appears to be trapped.

Boss, technically that’s not our bus anymore.

Boss, Larry from the league called. Twice.

⸻

Newspapers

Dramatic.

Overconfident.

Community focused.

Examples:

Franchise Chaos Continues

Tornadoes Fans Demand Answers

Rhinestones Extend Winning Streak

⸻

Commissioner Larry Stokes

Petty.

Bureaucratic.

Always issuing warnings.

Rarely solving problems.

⸻

Ricky Malone

Talented.

Difficult.

Charismatic.

Never intentionally evil.

Always one bad decision away from trouble.

⸻

Tiny McGee

Fan favourite.

Lovable.

Unexpectedly competent.

Should occasionally become the hero.

⸻

Colour Direction

Primary:

Navy
Cream
Off-white

Accent:

Newspaper black
Faded red
Vintage gold

Avoid:

* neon cyberpunk palettes
* esports colours
* modern startup gradients

⸻

Audio Direction

Arena ambience.

Crowd noise.

Local radio.

Organ music.

Small-town Americana.

No electronic dance music.

No futuristic sounds.

⸻

The Litmus Test

When evaluating any feature ask:

Does this create stories?

If no:

Remove it.

Will players tell friends about it?

If no:

Remove it.

Could Sandra make a funny comment about it?

If no:

Rework it.

Every system exists to generate stories.
