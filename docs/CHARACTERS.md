CHARACTERS

Design Rule

Characters are systems.

Every major character must:

* Generate stories
* Create decisions
* Cause problems
* Remember history
* Change over time

If a character cannot create stories, remove them.

⸻

Sandra S.

Role

Secretary.

Former sports reporter.

Narrator.

Advisor.

Keeper of franchise history.

Heart of the game.

⸻

Description

Sandra has seen everything.

She joined the franchise believing it would be temporary.

Ten years later she’s still here.

She quietly keeps the organization alive while everyone else creates chaos.

⸻

Personality

Dry.

Observant.

Loyal.

Patient.

Secretly ambitious for the franchise.

Never dramatic.

The world is ridiculous.

Sandra is the only person who notices.

⸻

Gameplay Purpose

Sandra delivers:

* weekly briefings
* event introductions
* consequences
* callbacks
* season memories

She comments on past decisions.

Examples:

Boss, this is our third mascot injury this month.

Boss, Ricky says buses are bad for his shooting rhythm.

Boss, the roof repair appears to have been mostly cosmetic.

⸻

Long-Term Arc

Sandra slowly becomes emotionally invested in the team’s success.

Players should care about making Sandra proud.

Role

Star Player

⸻

Description

Personality

Running Gag

Reasons change constantly.

Examples:

* bad luck
* suspicious upholstery
* bus cursed
* seat too close to emergency exit
* dreams told him not to

⸻

Gameplay Purpose

* player drama
* fan reactions
* media stories
* difficult choices

⸻

Possible Arcs

* Franchise hero
* Local celebrity
* League suspension
* Trade demand
* Redemption story
* Association call-up

The Association Scout

Role

The Dream

⸻

Description

A mysterious observer.

Appears unexpectedly.

Rarely speaks.

Never explains anything.

⸻

Gameplay Purpose

Represents advancement.

Creates pressure.

Changes behaviour of everyone around him.

⸻

Running Gag

Nobody is ever sure whether it’s actually the scout.

Examples:

A man in a trench coat attended the game.

Sandra believes it was the scout.

Hank believes it was a plumber.

Franchise Mascot

Design Rule

The mascot is effectively a recurring character.

The mascot will suffer injuries at an alarming rate.

⸻

Typical Incidents

* torn felt hamstring
* ladder accident
* stuck in rafters
* t-shirt cannon incident
* mascot collision
* parade injury
* beer night accident

⸻

Gameplay Purpose

Creates:

* comedy
* expenses
* newspaper stories
* community reactions

⸻

The City

The city itself is a character.

Fans remember events.

Businesses react.

Sponsors react.

Local newspapers react.

Nothing disappears completely.

History matters.

Every season should feel remembered.
