EVENTS

Core Principle

Events are the engine of the game.

Every week should contain at least one meaningful story event.

Players should never feel like they are repeating administrative tasks.

The event should be the memory.

⸻

Event Categories

Franchise Events

Problems affecting the organization.

Examples:

* arena roof leaks
* unpaid bills
* broken scoreboard
* sponsor disputes
* equipment shortages
* parking disasters

⸻

Player Events

Character-driven stories.

Examples:

* Ricky misses the bus
* Tiny adopts a stray dog
* player contract dispute
* locker room argument
* local celebrity appearance

⸻

Coach Events

Stories involving Hank Willis.

Examples:

* controversial quote
* punishment training session
* motivational stunt
* media backlash

⸻

League Events

Stories involving Commissioner Larry Stokes.

Examples:

* investigation
* fine
* warning letter
* rule change
* surprise inspection

⸻

Community Events

Small-town stories.

Examples:

* charity game
* county fair
* parade appearance
* school visit
* sponsor fundraiser

⸻

Mascot Events

Mandatory recurring category.

The mascot should be involved in a surprising number of incidents.

Examples:

* stuck in rafters
* t-shirt cannon malfunction
* mascot race injury
* costume theft
* parade collision

⸻

Association Events

Rare.

Important.

Exciting.

Examples:

* scout sighting
* player recommendation
* promotion rumours
* league evaluation

⸻

Event Structure

Every event follows:

Setup

Sandra introduces situation.

Example:

Boss, Ricky is refusing to board the team bus again.

⸻

Choice

Usually 2–4 options.

Example:

A. Force Ricky onto the bus.

B. Rent him a car.

C. Ignore it.

⸻

Consequence

Immediate result.

Sometimes hidden future consequences.

⸻

Memory

Event enters franchise history.

Future events may reference it.

⸻

Event Quality Test

Every event must satisfy at least one:

* Funny
* Emotional
* Memorable
* Difficult Decision
* Character Development

If not:

Delete it.

Recurring Storylines

Events should chain together.

Example:

Week 4:
Roof leak discovered.

Week 8:
Repair delayed.

Week 13:
Leak damages scoreboard.

Week 20:
Arena flooded during game.

Week 31:
Insurance investigation.

One story should create future stories.

⸻

Long-Term Goal

The player should finish a season with a believable franchise history.

Not:

Revenue increased 8%.

Instead:

Ricky got suspended.

The mascot broke both ankles.

We survived three investigations.

Tiny became a city icon.

Somehow we made the playoffs.
